# Business Recommendations Report
## E-Commerce Sales & Customer Analytics — GlobalMart

**Prepared by:** Data Analytics Team  
**Date:** 2024  
**Dataset Period:** 2011–2014  

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis covers 51,290 orders across 7 global markets and 3 product categories.
Total revenue exceeded $12.6M with a profit of approximately $1.47M (11.7% margin).
While the business shows year-over-year growth in sales, profit margins are being compressed by aggressive discounting — particularly in the Furniture category.

---

## Finding 1 — Discounts Are Destroying Profit in Furniture

**Observation:**  
The Furniture category — especially Tables and Bookcases — shows negative total profit despite significant sales volume.

**Insight:**  
Orders with discounts above 20% consistently result in negative profit margins. The average discount on Tables is over 30%, meaning the company is selling Tables at a loss.

**Business Recommendation:**  
- Cap the maximum discount on Tables and Bookcases at 10%.  
- Require manager approval for any discount above 15% on Furniture.  
- Run a 90-day test: remove discounts on bottom-margin Furniture items and monitor volume impact.

---

## Finding 2 — Technology Drives Profit, Office Supplies Drives Volume

**Observation:**  
Technology has the highest profit margin (~14%) despite not having the highest revenue. Office Supplies has the highest order count but thin margins.

**Insight:**  
The business is cross-subsidising — Office Supplies volume supports shipping economics, while Technology earns the profit.

**Business Recommendation:**  
- Increase marketing investment in Technology (Phones, Copiers).  
- Bundle Office Supplies with Technology products to increase average order value.  
- Do not discount Technology products — they are already selling well with healthy margins.

---

## Finding 3 — Central Region is Underperforming

**Observation:**  
The Central region has solid sales but the lowest profit margin across all US regions.

**Insight:**  
High discount rates in Central region combined with higher shipping costs are eroding margin.

**Business Recommendation:**  
- Conduct a discount audit specifically for Central region sales reps.  
- Review shipping carrier contracts for Central region routes.  
- Set region-specific discount caps and track margin by rep.

---

## Finding 4 — Standard Class Shipping Is Underused for Large Orders

**Observation:**  
First Class and Same Day shipping modes account for a disproportionate share of shipping costs relative to their order value.

**Insight:**  
Customers are choosing premium shipping for low-value orders, increasing cost without meaningful revenue impact.

**Business Recommendation:**  
- Set a minimum order value threshold (e.g., $100) for First Class shipping eligibility.  
- Offer free Standard Class shipping above $150 to incentivise customers to self-select slower, cheaper shipping.

---

## Finding 5 — Top 20% of Customers Generate 80% of Revenue

**Observation:**  
A small group of Corporate and Consumer segment customers account for the majority of lifetime revenue.

**Insight:**  
Classic Pareto principle applies. The business has a highly concentrated revenue base, which creates risk if these customers churn.

**Business Recommendation:**  
- Build a VIP loyalty programme for Platinum and Gold tier customers.  
- Assign dedicated account managers to top 50 customers.  
- Introduce early access offers for repeat customers to improve retention.

---

## Finding 6 — Seasonality Peak in Q4 (Oct–Dec)

**Observation:**  
Sales consistently spike in Q4 across all years in the dataset.

**Insight:**  
Q4 is the revenue engine. Q1 and Q2 are significantly weaker.

**Business Recommendation:**  
- Pre-stock inventory for Q4 by September.  
- Launch retention campaigns in Q1 to reduce the post-holiday revenue dip.  
- Test a mid-year promotional event (e.g., mid-year sale) to flatten the seasonality curve.
